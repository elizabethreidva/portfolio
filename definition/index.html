What is a Design Technologist?

Design technologists have expertise that combines the conceptual creative thinking of a designer with the technical skills of a front-end developer. We have hands-on knowledge of one or more delivery mediums, but also experience working in UX design concepts. We bring technical knowledge to advise designers on how their designs will be implemented, and help developers understand design intent to ensure successful translation to delivery.

Design Technologist vs Design Engineer

I've gone back and forth on this, because these titles are sometimes used interchangeably. The definitions are still emerging across the software industry. The confusion increases when you start rubbing elbows with non-software engineering, e.g. mechanical, electrical, and so on.

To borrow from Google's AI Overview

“Design technologists focus on the practical implementation and technical aspects of a design, while design engineers are more involved in the conceptualization, analysis, and overall feasibility of a design.”

The AI Overview also offers this side-by-side comparison:

  Design Technologist Design Engineer
Focus Focus on practical implementation, technical feasibility, and user experience. Focus on conceptualization, analysis, and overall feasibility of a design.
Responsibilities
  • Building prototypes and testing designs.
  • Ensuring designs are accessible and performant.
  • Collaborating with designers and engineers to translate designs into functional products.
  • Working with code and technology to create interactive experiences.
  • May be involved in creating design systems and style guides.
  • Developing design concepts and solutions.
  • Analyzing various aspects of problems to create the best solutions.
  • Using software programs to design concepts and prototypes.
  • Ensuring designs meet functional and technical requirements.
  • May be involved in testing and refining designs.
Skills Strong coding skills, knowledge of design principles, understanding of user experience, and ability to translate designs into code. Strong analytical and problem-solving skills, knowledge of engineering principles, and proficiency in design software.

Based on this summary, I lean more towards design engineer. I want to be a voice in the complete design process, not just how the design gets built. That said, because of the confusion with non-software engineering, I'm seeing a shift towards design technologist as the more common role title. So that's what I'll be using to tell my story.
